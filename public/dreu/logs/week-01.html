Week 01 Research Log

Dates

June 8, 2026 - June 14, 2026

Goals

The goal for this week was to get oriented in the DREU research program, begin settling into the research environment at UIUC, and start building the background needed for our summer project. Since the project is focused on quantum algorithms and factoring, I wanted to spend this first week understanding the broader research direction before jumping too quickly into implementation.

I also wanted to attend group activities and meetings so I could better understand the lab culture, current research discussions, and how our project fits into the larger research environment.

Approach and Implementation

This week was mostly focused on learning, orientation, and gathering context. I attended a Lunch and Learn session, which helped introduce me to the broader research community and gave me a better sense of how students and researchers talk about their work in a structured setting.

I also attended the main meeting for the Parasol Lab. This was really interesting because the lab works on a lot of exciting systems-related research, including robotics, motion planning, and other areas that connect theory with real-world computational problems. One of the talks discussed motion planning, which stood out to me because it showed how algorithmic ideas become much more complicated and interesting when they have to work in physical environments. Even though my own project is focused on quantum algorithms, seeing research on robotics and planning helped me think more broadly about how theoretical computer science, optimization, and implementation challenges show up across different areas of computing.

Our group is still waiting for one of our research partners to fully join, but he was able to share notes with us. These notes helped us begin aligning on what background material we should study and what topics may become important for the project. I began reviewing introductory quantum computing concepts, including qubits, gates, circuits, measurement, and the general role of the quantum Fourier transform in Shor's algorithm.

Results

By the end of the week, I had a stronger understanding of the research environment and the expectations for weekly progress. I became more familiar with the lab meeting structure, started building the background knowledge needed for the project, and began identifying the main technical topics I will need to study more deeply.

The Parasol Lab meeting also made the research environment feel more concrete to me. It was helpful to see people presenting active research problems instead of only reading papers or background material on my own. The robotics and motion planning discussion especially gave me a better sense of how broad the lab's work is and how much of research involves taking an abstract idea and figuring out how to make it actually work.

The most important outcome from this week was that I now have a clearer starting point: before working on the full factoring algorithm, I need to understand the quantum Fourier transform, inverse quantum Fourier transform, period finding, and how these pieces connect to Shor's algorithm. Next week, I plan to continue studying these concepts and begin moving toward implementation-based exploration.
